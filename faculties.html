The faculties and departments of the university form the academic core of the institution. Our engineering school offers undergraduate, postgraduate, and professional programs designed to equip students with both theoretical knowledge and practical skills.

Mechanical Engineering

Department I

Mechanical Engineering is a dynamic and versatile field that forms the backbone of many modern industries. It involves the design, analysis, manufacturing, and maintenance of mechanical systems — from tiny microdevices to large-scale machinery like turbines and engines.

Thermodynamics Fluid Mechanics Solid Mechanics Materials Science Dynamics & Control CAD & Manufacturing

Career opportunities for mechanical engineers are vast — graduates work in automotive engineering, aerospace design, power generation, robotics, renewable energy, biomedical devices, and AI-driven mechanical systems.

Civil Engineering

Department II

Civil Engineering is all about designing, building, and maintaining the physical and naturally built environment. It's the field responsible for much of the infrastructure we rely on every day — roads, bridges, airports, tunnels, water systems, and buildings.

Structural Engineering Geotechnical Engineering Transportation Engineering Hydraulics & Water Resources Environmental Engineering AutoCAD & GIS Tools
Civil Engineering students during practical sessions

Electrical Engineering

Department III

Electrical Engineering is at the core of the digital revolution. It covers everything from power generation and transmission to electronics, computing, and communications — powering homes and industries while enabling smart technologies.

Circuit Theory Digital Systems Microprocessors Control & Automation Power Systems Telecommunications Signal Processing

Electrical engineers are in high demand in telecommunications, aerospace, renewable energy, electronics design, embedded systems, and smart city technologies. This course also builds a strong foundation for robotics, AI, and IoT.
